Huevos Ahogados

Description

These Mexican-style country potatoes are my go-to when I want something super flavorful but easy to make. They’re crispy on the outside, soft inside, and loaded with sautéed onions, bell peppers, tomatoes, and a little kick from serrano peppers. I love the smoky vibe from the paprika and cumin, and the fresh herbs just bring it all together. Whether I’m having them for breakfast or as a side for dinner, these potatoes always hit the spot!

Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 tomatoes, diced (or 1 can diced tomatoes)
  • 1-2 serrano peppers, finely chopped (adjust for spice level)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • Tortillas or crusty bread for serving

Steps

  1. Sauté the veggies: In a large pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened. Add the minced garlic and serrano peppers, cooking for another minute until fragrant.
  2. Make the sauce: Add the diced tomatoes to the pan and cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally until the tomatoes break down and form a thick sauce. Season with cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Pour in the water or vegetable broth and bring to a simmer.
  3. Poach the eggs: Once the sauce is simmering, crack the eggs directly into the sauce, making small wells for each egg. Cover the pan with a lid and let the eggs cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until the whites are set but the yolks are still runny (or cook longer for firmer yolks).
  4. Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve the huevos ahogados with warm tortillas or crusty bread for dipping. Enjoy this delicious and comforting dish while it’s hot!
